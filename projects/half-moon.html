Project Overview

A comprehensive portfolio of design and project management work for Half Moon Empanadas across multiple airport locations in the United States. These projects involved conceptual layout design, massing, equipment selection, 3D modeling, signage design, and overseeing the construction phase as project manager. The kiosks were designed to attract travelers in busy airport environments while optimizing operations in a high-volume, fast-paced setting.

Washington DCA

Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport

The Washington DCA location design involved creating a vibrant, eye-catching kiosk to attract travelers in the busy airport environment. The bold pink color scheme and geometric patterns were chosen to make the brand stand out, while the layout was optimized for efficiency in a high-volume, fast-paced setting.

As the project manager during construction, I coordinated between multiple vendors including millwork fabricators, signage manufacturers, equipment suppliers, IT specialists, and general contractors. Weekly progress meetings were established to ensure all parties were aligned and that the project remained on schedule. I managed the bidding process for selecting the general contractor, evaluating proposals based on cost, timeline, and quality of work.

Denver DEN

Denver International Airport

The Denver International Airport location incorporates a unique bike-themed design element while maintaining brand consistency. This location was designed to accommodate higher traffic volumes with an expanded preparation area and service counter. The distinctive branding and layout ensure visibility in the busy concourse environment.

For this location, we incorporated local Denver culture by integrating a bicycle theme into the design, creating a memorable visual feature that would resonate with the local audience while maintaining the core Half Moon Empanadas brand identity. The layout was optimized for faster service times during peak airport hours.
